Electronic waste or e-waste describes discarded electrical or electronic devices. Used electronics which are destined for reuse, resale, salvage, recycling, or disposal are also considered e-waste.

Informal processing of e-waste in developing countries can lead to adverse human health effects and environmental pollution.

Electronics for consumers are being treated like disposable items more and more every day.

One major factor causing this trend is that companies are endlessly marketing new technologies to us, which effectively render recent products as obsolete.

Electronic waste or e-waste describes discarded electrical or electronic devices. Used electronics which are destined for refurbishment, reuse, resale, salvage recycling through material recovery, or disposal are also considered e-waste. In last few decades, in search of making human life easier day by day and due to advancement in the field of electronics and technology have result in exponential increase in the category of E-waste. From the time when first computer was invented we have travelled very far ahead an now living in the world in which mostly every sector of work is directly and indirectly related to phones, laptops, and many others electronics gadgets for making our day to day life easier and for saving our time. E-waste can be harmful for health if not managed carefully as many electronic devices contains of harmful substances such as lead, cadmium, beryllium, etc. China is leading in the list of countries that generates highest amount of E-waste as it produces lot of electronic stuff as well as being the most populated country in the world it also consumes a lot more electronic devices. The list further contains countries such as USA , Japan and even India is also a major source of E-waste due to its vast population. Several countries mostly under developed countries don't have a proper plan for recycling their E-waste. According to Slade, Giles. "iWaste." Mother Jones, 2007. Web Accessed April 11, 2015, every year approximately 50 million metric tones of E-waste is generated across the world and out of which 20% or even less amount of E-waste is recycled. Such large increasing number E-waste is really a matter of concern for every country. According to U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. "Fact Sheet: MANAGEMENT OF ELECTRONIC WASTE IN THE UNITED STATES." Web Accessed April 11, 2015, till now only 12.5% of E-waste is recycled across the world.
